
# Customer Discovery Interview Guide

## Project Overview

This interview guide was developed as part of the **ICT105 Course Project** to conduct customer discovery research. The objective is to understand how people currently discover movies, the challenges they face when choosing what to watch, and whether a movie recommendation system could help improve their experience.

The insights gathered from these interviews will help identify user pain points and validate whether a digital movie recommendation solution would effectively address the problem.

---

# Research Objective

The purpose of this interview is to:

* Understand how users currently choose movies to watch.
* Identify existing methods used to discover new movies.
* Discover pain points, frustrations, and inefficiencies in the movie selection process.
* Validate whether a movie recommendation system would provide value to users.

---

# Research Method

**Research Type:** Customer Discovery Interview

**Target Participants:** University students, movie enthusiasts, and streaming platform users

**Interview Format:** Semi-structured interview

**Estimated Duration:** 5–10 minutes

**Data Collection Method:**

* Interview notes
* Optional Google Form responses
* Anonymous qualitative feedback

---

# Interview Questions

## Section 1 — Consent and Introduction

1. May we ask you a few questions for our ICT105 course project?

2. Your participation is completely voluntary. We will only collect information relevant to this research, and your identity will remain anonymous.

3. Do you consent to participating in this interview and allowing us to take notes?

---

## Section 2 — Understanding the User's Experience

4. Can you describe how you usually decide what movie to watch?

5. What do you normally look for when choosing a movie?

6. Could you walk us through the last time you spent time searching for a movie to watch?

---

## Section 3 — Current Solutions and Behaviors

7. How do you currently find movie recommendations?

8. What resources do you usually rely on?

   * Friends and family
   * Social media
   * Google search
   * Streaming platform recommendations
   * Movie review websites
   * Other methods

9. What aspects of the current process do you find difficult, slow, confusing, or frustrating?

---

## Section 4 — Problem Validation

10. How often do you have difficulty finding a movie that interests you?

* Rarely
* Occasionally
* Frequently
* Very Frequently

11. On a scale of **1–5**, how serious do you consider this problem?

Please explain the reason for your rating.

12. What happens when you cannot find a suitable movie to watch?

Examples may include:

* Wasting time searching
* Feeling frustrated
* Watching a movie you do not enjoy
* Rewatching old movies
* Giving up on watching altogether

---

## Section 5 — Closing Questions

13. If you could improve the way movies are recommended to you, what would you change?

14. What features would you like to see in a movie recommendation system?

15. May we use your anonymous responses as supporting evidence in our ICT105 project report?

---

# Participant Information

* Participation is voluntary.
* You may skip any question or stop the interview at any time.
* Your responses will remain anonymous.

---

# What We Hope to Learn

From these interviews, we hope to understand:

* How users currently discover and select movies.
* The biggest challenges in finding suitable movies.
* What users need from a better movie recommendation system.
* Whether personalized recommendations would improve the movie-watching experience.
