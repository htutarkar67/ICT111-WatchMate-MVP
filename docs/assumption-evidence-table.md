
# Assumption-Evidence Table

|  No.  | Team Assumption                                                                                           | Evidence Collected                                                                                                                                                                                     | Evidence Type | Strength | Decision                                                                           |
| :---: | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ | ------------- | -------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **1** | Users spend too much time searching for movies and struggle to find content that matches their interests. | Interviews with 22 respondents showed that most users spend a significant amount of time browsing streaming platforms before selecting a movie, and many reported difficulty finding suitable content. | Interview     | Strong   | **Keep** (Validates the core problem of movie discovery and recommendation).       |
| **2** | Existing recommendation features on streaming platforms are not sufficiently personalized for many users. | Several respondents stated that platform recommendations often repeat similar content or suggest movies that do not match their preferences.                                                           | Interview     | Moderate | **Keep** (Supports the need for a more personalized recommendation approach).      |
| **3** | Users would be willing to provide their movie preferences to receive better recommendations.              | Most respondents indicated they would select favorite genres, actors, or movie categories if it helped them receive more accurate recommendations.                                                     | Interview     | Strong   | **Keep** (Confirms that preference-based recommendations are acceptable to users). |

### Why these make sense for our project:

* **Assumption 1** validates the primary problem that users face when choosing movies from large streaming libraries. It confirms that movie discovery is time-consuming and often frustrating.

* **Assumption 2** evaluates whether current alternatives already solve the problem. The evidence suggests that existing recommendation systems do not always provide relevant suggestions, creating an opportunity for improvement.

* **Assumption 3** tests whether users are willing to interact with the system by providing preference information. The positive response supports the design of a personalized recommendation feature in the MVP.
